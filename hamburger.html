Hamburger

Prep: 15 mins | ♨️ Calories: 650 kcal | 🍲 Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • The Meat: 400g freshly ground beef (80% lean, 20% fat), divided into 4 loosely packed balls.
  • The Buns: 2 soft Brioche or potato buns, split and buttered.
  • The Cheese: 4 slices of classic American cheese (melts perfectly).
  • The Sauce: 2 tbsp mayonnaise, 1 tbsp ketchup, 1 tsp sweet relish (mix them together).
  • The Toppings: Thinly sliced white onions, dill pickles, and shredded iceberg lettuce.
  • Seasoning: Coarse sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper.

Instructions

    1. Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat until it's smoking hot.
    2. Toast the buttered sides of the buns in the skillet until golden brown, then set them aside.
    3. Place the beef balls onto the hot skillet. Immediately place a piece of parchment paper over each ball and use a heavy spatula to *smash* them flat and thin.
    4. Season the smashed patties generously with salt and pepper. Cook undisturbed for about 2 minutes until a dark, crusty edge forms.
    5. Scrape tightly under the patties and flip them. Immediately place a slice of American cheese on each patty.
    6. Cook for 1 more minute until the cheese is completely melted. Stack two patties on top of each other.
    7. Assemble the burger: Spread the secret sauce on the bottom bun, add lettuce, pickles, the double cheeseburger patties, and top with the bun. Serve hot!
